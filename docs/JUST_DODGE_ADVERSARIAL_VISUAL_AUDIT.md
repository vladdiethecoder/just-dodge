# Just Dodge — Adversarial Visual and Technical Audit

**Audit date:** 2026-07-20
**Scope:** all evidence supplied in this conversation: five current character/rig stills, prior game-loop observer/“first-person” captures, the Blender impact-rig evidence package, the proposed mythic dotted arena direction, and the proposed PhysGen residual-motion architecture.
**Decision rule:** visible proof only. A screenshot does not prove hidden topology, weights, determinism, runtime cost, legal provenance, or animation robustness.

## Executive verdict

The current build is a technically promising **diagnostic prototype**, not a visually promotable full game. Three claims must be corrected immediately:

1. **The full character kit is not near production-ready.** The two rejected bone-parent captures show catastrophic transform/weight failures; the full-kit capture has a destroyed sword hand. These are hard rejects, not polish issues.
2. **The arena proposal is a valid art-direction hypothesis, not an implementation-ready specification.** A generated dotted-painting skybox plus realistic sand can easily become stylistically split, seam-prone, shimmering, high-contrast visual noise unless it is converted into an original, measurable visual grammar.
3. **The PhysGen proposal is a research program, not a proven runtime architecture.** MotionBricks and ARDY are credible research references, but neither proves deterministic two-character contact, 120 Hz end-to-end delivery, or a safe post-decode projection seam for Just Dodge.

I did **not** invent 1,000 unique defects. The supplied stills cannot honestly support that claim. Instead, Appendix A contains exactly **1,000 traceable audit cells** (5 images × 25 screen-space regions × 8 criteria). Each cell is classified as `FAIL_VISIBLE`, `PASS_IN_THIS_VIEW`, or `UNPROVEN_FROM_SINGLE_FRONT_VIEW`, with a concrete closure action. This resolves the requested scale without falsifying evidence.

## What “resolved” means here

The PNG evidence is read-only. It is impossible to repair armature weights, parenting, topology or sockets inside a screenshot. Therefore:

- **Audit resolution:** complete — every supplied character view is classified and assigned a closure action in the 1,000-cell matrix.
- **Asset resolution:** blocked on source — actual repairs require the canonical `.blend` or FBX/GLB, its armature, actions, armor meshes and weapon object.
- **Promotion resolution:** fail-closed — the failed full-kit and bone-parent outputs stay quarantined until new evidence passes the gates below.

## Character/rig recovery order

1. Freeze the clean body and canonical armature; apply/verify scale, rotation and object transforms before binding anything.
2. Remove duplicate armatures, duplicate armature modifiers and stale vertex groups. Confirm exactly one intended deform chain per limb.
3. Rebind the body first. Validate shoulders, elbows, wrists and every finger in a neutral pose and five stress poses.
4. Split equipment by behavior: rigid sword and hard plates use sockets/constraints; cloth/glove/underlayer uses controlled skinning; articulated armor uses explicit rigid segments or dedicated bones.
5. Refit vambraces in rest space. Never compensate a wrong parent inverse by painting more weights.
6. Build a named weapon-grip socket and calibrate position/orientation locally. The sword must not be skinned to hand vertices.
7. Restore hand topology, then repaint finger/wrist weights with normalized influence limits. Test fist, open hand, pronation/supination, guard and sword grip.
8. Run automated checks: non-finite vertices, unexpected AABB growth, unweighted vertices, weight normalization, cross-limb influences, duplicate deform modifiers and object-scale mismatches.
9. Render a proof package: front/back/left/right/top, wireframe, weight heatmaps, five deformation poses, sword-grip close-up and a 360° turntable.
10. Import the exact candidate into wgpu and repeat the proof at gameplay FOV and near clip. Blender-only success is insufficient.

### Proposed hard gates

These are new proposed gates, not claims about the current asset:

- No duplicate limbs, non-finite vertices, unexpected vertex spikes or transform-driven AABB explosion.
- No visible body/armor penetration in the approved pose suite and 360° turntable.
- Weapon handle remains centered in the intended grip and preserves its calibrated orientation throughout attacks, blocks and recovery.
- No cross-side deform influence (left-hand vertices driven by right-arm bones or vice versa) unless explicitly documented and tested.
- Hands preserve palm volume and finger ordering in open, fist and weapon-grip poses.
- First-person camera never enters face, torso or opponent geometry in the gameplay envelope.

## Arena direction — adversarial judgment

The “central sand island + water + divine classical architecture + dotted old-school painting” direction is compatible with the game, but the optimistic implementation plan is incomplete.

### Keep

- A clear, low-frequency sand combat surface.
- Water and distant architecture as strong depth layers.
- Mythic light, clouds and restrained pointillist/dither vocabulary.
- A deliberately sparse central arena so footwork and intent silhouettes remain primary.

### Reject or constrain

- Do not ask a generator to copy the linked artist/post “exactly.” Build an original style grammar from broad traits: pointillist texture, classical scale, limited palette, radiant atmosphere and monumental silhouettes.
- Do not use one generated panorama as final world art. It will expose seams, inconsistent vanishing points, lighting mismatch and absent parallax.
- Do not apply full-screen dither uniformly. It will shimmer under camera motion, damage thin limbs/weapon edges and corrupt HUD legibility.
- Do not mix photoreal sand and painterly surroundings without a shared palette, roughness range and atmospheric treatment; the result can look like two unrelated games composited together.
- Do not let bright clouds or architecture sit directly behind fighters. Preserve a stable mid-value combat backdrop and test silhouette contrast continuously.

### Production prescription

Use true 3D for the island, water, nearest columns/ruins and reflection receivers; reserve cards/cubemap for distant clouds and horizons. Apply world-stable stipple to distant materials, temporal-stable color quantization after anti-aliasing, and exclude UI plus critical contact silhouettes from the stylization mask. Treat the sky reference as concept art; rebuild lighting and depth in-engine.

Arena acceptance requires: seam-free 360° camera motion, stable temporal dots, readable fighters at every legal position, no horizon/reflection mismatch, no UI degradation, and acceptable frame time on the target platform.

## PhysGen proposal — claim audit

| Claim | Evidence-backed judgment |
|---|---|
| MotionBricks is a real modular generative-motion system | Supported. NVIDIA describes a modular latent generative backbone, smart primitives and real-time operation; its reported research performance does not prove Just Dodge integration. |
| ARDY supports interactive constraints | Supported. It accepts text, root paths/waypoints, full-body keyframes and sparse joint constraints. |
| ARDY is ready for a deterministic 120 Hz combat loop | Unsupported. The paper reports roughly 33 ms average four-step generation and identifies multi-step diffusion cost, long-history inefficiency, kinematic-only motion, foot skating and jitter as limitations. |
| Depth Anything V2 yields clean skeletal motion/contact truth | Unsupported. It estimates monocular depth; depth is an auxiliary teacher signal, not bone identity, metric contact or temporal combat truth. |
| Seedance proves an engine-ready martial-arts transfer path | Unsupported. A generated video can be visual reference, but does not provide a lawful, retargetable, contact-valid skeletal sequence. Current Seedance 2.0 copyright disputes also make provenance discipline essential. |
| Soft post-decode projection safely reconciles generated motion with truth | Unproven and risky. Large corrections create skating, popping, penetration and misleading silhouettes. The generator must be conditioned on explicit constraints and the candidate must pass a verifier; projection cannot be the primary rescue mechanism. |
| Presentation may diverge freely because truth remains deterministic | Too broad. If the player sees contact that truth did not register—or misses contact truth did register—the game becomes visually dishonest. Presentation-only freedom belongs in camera, particles, trails, lighting and non-contact secondary motion, not contact-critical hands, feet, weapons or roots. |

### Corrected architecture

1. Deterministic truth emits a versioned, quantized condition packet: intent, phase, root trajectory, required contacts, forbidden penetrations, injury/limb mask and actionability horizon.
2. The asynchronous motion provider generates candidates without ever blocking or feeding back into truth.
3. A fail-closed verifier measures root/contact/clearance/limb-state error. Only admissible candidates may drive contact-critical presentation.
4. Minor residual correction is limited to a documented envelope. A large rescue is rejection, not projection.
5. Camera/VFX/audio juice derives from deterministic truth events. Any hitstop that changes gameplay time belongs in truth; a purely visual freeze must not misrepresent continuing simulation.
6. Depth/video data is quarantined as weak teacher/reference material with explicit rights and provenance. It never becomes ground truth merely because it looks plausible.

The current attached Grab-07 evaluation already demonstrates why this matters: the supplied report records a **FAIL**, with approximately 300.139 mm median hand distance, 912.334 mm p95, 1518.589 mm worst case and 65.994 mm foot sliding. That is not a polish gap and cannot be hidden by a visual residual.
