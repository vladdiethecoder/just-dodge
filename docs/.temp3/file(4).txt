Simulation Method: Finite Element Method (FEM) — corotational tetrahedral
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ELASTIC ZONE       → Force < yield strength (200–250 MPa)
                     Plate vibrates, rings, returns to shape
                     Audio: metallic ring proportional to force

PLASTIC ZONE       → Force exceeds yield strength
                     Permanent dent forms at impact site
                     Dent depth = f(mass × velocity²) / area
                     Dented plate: reduced coverage, stress concentrator

PIERCING THRESHOLD → Pierce stress > ultimate strength / tip area
                     Weapon tip punches through → hole + petal deform
                     Petals fold inward → secondary wound geometry

SHEAR / CLEAVE     → Lateral force along plate edge
                     Plate edge curls, buckles, or severs
                     Severed plate section becomes physics object

BLUNT (war hammer) → Force below pierce threshold
                     Plate dents inward → contacts bone/flesh
                     "Transmission trauma" — bone fracture under intact plate
                     Plate integrity reduced, joint ROM changes from deformation

CUMULATIVE dents   → Stress concentration at dent sites
                     Subsequent hits at same site → fracture threshold -40%
                     Plate eventually shatters at fracture point

HALF-SWORDING      → Grip on blade = blunt force application to plate gaps
                     Concentrated force on joint seams → pry/lever open
