# Just Dodge — Viral Readiness Checklist

## Scoring

Score each category 1–5:
- **5:** Strong, demonstrable strength.
- **4:** Good, minor gaps.
- **3:** Acceptable but needs work before launch.
- **2:** Significant risk; may block viability.
- **1:** Critical weakness; likely to fail.

**Strong launch candidate:** Average 4+ with no category below 3.

---

## 1. Hook
**Question:** Can someone understand the appeal in 5 seconds?

**Score:** 4

A first-person simultaneous-reveal duel is visually and conceptually clear. The "I knew you would do that" moment is easy to show. The title "Just Dodge" slightly undersells the depth; marketing copy should emphasize the read/reveal loop and For Honor-level physicality.

## 2. Feel
**Question:** Is the core action fun with no rewards?

**Score:** 3 (pending prototype)

The YOMI triangle is proven fun in other games, but this implementation must prove it with its own timing, MotionBricks motion, and hitbox parity. Shape/First Playable prototypes will validate.

## 3. Replay
**Question:** Do players ask for another round/session?

**Score:** 4

Short duels (1–5 minutes) encourage rematches. The mind-game loop naturally drives "one more match." Local 2P and rivalries amplify this.

## 4. Watchability
**Question:** Can a spectator follow the stakes?

**Score:** 4

The reveal-to-consequence sequence is short and legible. Deep material and injury consequences give spectators clear stakes. First-person camera risks hiding some tells; camera design must stay readable.

## 5. Shareability
**Question:** Does the game produce clips/screenshots/stories?

**Score:** 5

Every match can produce a clip-worthy exchange. Fight Film auto-generates cinematic highlights. Deep armor damage and injury consequences are visually distinctive and replayable. Replay files enable exact sharing.

## 6. Social Pull
**Question:** Is it better with friends or comparison?

**Score:** 4

Local 2P, shared replays, and fight films create social moments. Online ranked/direct-IP adds competitive pull post-launch.

## 7. Novelty
**Question:** Does it have a fresh twist?

**Score:** 4

Simultaneous-reveal dueling exists (YOMI Hustle, OATHYARD). Combining it with For Honor-level first-person fidelity, deep material simulation, and perfect hitbox parity is a strong differentiator if executed well.

## 8. Depth
**Question:** Is there mastery beyond the first session?

**Score:** 5

13-action matrix, stance/tempo, deep armor counterplay, tissue injury consequences, weapon families, and AI personalities provide extensive mastery paths. Risk: complexity may overwhelm novices without strong tutorialization.

## 9. Discovery
**Question:** Does the platform know who to recommend it to?

**Score:** 3

Steam tags (Fighting, Indie, Strategy, Action) are well-aligned. Short clips work on TikTok/Shorts. Needs a clear capsule and trailer to convert discovery.

## 10. Community
**Question:** Can players gather, feedback, and advocate?

**Score:** 3

Replay sharing and fight films give players something to discuss. Discord/community plan is needed before Closed Alpha.

## 11. Operations
**Question:** Can you patch, moderate, and scale?

**Score:** 2

Deterministic replay/hash makes regression testing strong, but deep material/injury solvers and hitbox parity create a large maintenance surface for a solo developer. Multiplayer and live ops must be deferred until local game is great.

## 12. Safety/Legal
**Question:** Are ratings, privacy, and moderation planned?

**Score:** 4

Melee violence and persistent wounds may affect rating, but no gore, gambling, or online toxicity vectors are designed in. PvP moderation plan needed for launch.
